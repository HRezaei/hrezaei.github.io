The Magic of Numpad

Once upon a time, I noticed that if we type two four-digit numbers according to a circle on the numpad (as the arrows show in the picture), and calculate their GCD, we will get a coefficient of 11, whatever those numbers be, and whatever direction (either clockwise or anticlockwise) we go through! it is also true for two eight-digit numbers, or even for a four-digit and an eight-digit one!

Try yourself:

I didn't find it mentioned anywhere and unfortunately I couldn't develop a proof for it. I also shared it with some mathematics students, but they didn't pay attention. So I sent an email to Professor Adleman (the A in the RSA algorithm!) and asked him about the reason behind this. I also asked the question "Isn't it worthy for further investigation?"

The answer is quoted below as it was:
Hi
I can give you a hint.
Write numbers base 10 and then consider the powers of 10 modulo 11.
Good luck

Professor Leonard Adleman
